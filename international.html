Introduction

The International Week School Project was a semester course event that brought together students from different countries in a full immersive experience. Through nine diverse groups, we worked under pressure to build interactive and impactful concepts, harnessing our cultural exchange and collaboration to create innovative solutions. This project challenged us to think outside the box, develop essential skills, and foster cross-cultural understanding, culminating in a transformative journey of personal growth and international cooperation.

Understanding the problem

Proudly and happily collaborating with Vestide and Woonbedrijf, our project aimed to develop an interactive tool to support the students of the LOT community, a community created by Vestide to enable students to voice themselves.

Goal: to create an innovative platform that would incentivize students to share their ideas and perspectives on the design and development of a new student residence at the university campus. By harnessing the power of technology, architecture and design we aimed to foster a sense of community engagement, allowing students to actively contribute to shaping their living environment.

Working flow

Working in a multicultural and multidisciplinary team comprised of students from Germany, Bulgaria, Mozambique, France, The Netherlands and Armenia our project followed a dynamic workflow. We kicked off with intense collective brainstorming sessions, leveraging our diverse backgrounds in media design, landscape design, and architecture. As the project progressed, tasks were distributed among team members. I had the honor of focusing on designing the user interface for the interactive tool, ensuring an intuitive and engaging user experience. Additionally, I contributed to the project by creating the tool's logo, adding a visual identity that captured the essence of our collaborative effort.

Concept

A space where students are allowed to speak up their minds to strengthen their community values while visualizing the contribution of their input on a bigger scale.

Input Screen

The loop video attached showcases the user flow of the Plot tool, allowing students to input their ideas. The video starts with a view displaying four quadrant buttons, representing the values of the LOT community. When a user clicks in one of the buttons to contribute their ideas, they are directed to a message field where they can leave their input. Finally, the video concludes with a confirmation and appreciation pop-up screen, where users have the option to provide their email if they wish to receive updates on the idea they shared. If you want to fully experience the prototype and navigate through the user flow, don't forget to check out the accompanying Figma file.

Reflections

The Dutch Design Charette was profound experience that left a lasting impact on me. Engaging with individuals from diverse nationalities not only broadened my horizons but also deepened my understanding of collaborative work. Through this immersive journey, I witnessed the power of effective communication and learned valuable lessons in teamwork. The experience itself was mesmerizing, but winning the charette was the icing on the cake, pleasantly surprising me and validating our hard work.

Some key takeaways from this project week include:

  • Enhanced understanding of collaborative work through engagement with people from different nationalities.
  • Improved communication skills, realizing the importance of effective and open dialogue.
  • Enjoying the perks of free food, adding a touch of fun and camaraderie to the experience.
  • Expanding our professional network, creating lasting connections for future collaborations.

With these lessons and memories in mind, I embrace the motto "Power to the people", recognizing the strength that lies within collaboration, diverse perspectives, and shared goals.
