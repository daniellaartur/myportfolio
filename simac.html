Introduction

During the current school semester, I collaborated with five other strong-minded women to improve SIMAC NL's onboarding process for international professionals coming to the Netherlands for work.
Our mission was to create a comprehensive onboarding program tailored to the unique needs of international hires. Through extensive research collaboration with SIMAC, and continuosly iterations, we developed an innovative product that not only addressed practical aspects but also emphasized cultural differences and encouraged new hires to step out of their comfort zone in a fun and engaging environment, challenging individuals to build relationships with their fellow coworkers.

Approaching the project

Setting up rules & roles

Our approach to the semester project was initially met with a sense of apprehension due to its vague nature. However, through the guidance and invaluable tips provided by our teachers, we quickly learned the importance of breaking things down and seeking a starting point.
As a team, we opted to leverage the power of Monday.com, a collaborative platform that facilitated task division and ensured fair assignment of work among us. One key takeaway from the planning stage was the initiative I took to be the contact person with stakeholders, fostering clear and effective communication among all parties involved. Understand

Research Question

How can SIMAC become more attractive to non-Dutch people?

The question above suggests that the current enviornment at SIMAC is rather exclusive, without huge variety in employee nationalities. The aim in the formulated collocation was to accept and embrance the broadness of the problem assigned while tailoring our research to a specific target group, international professionals.

Empathise stage

The primary empathize stage of the project focused on gaining a profound understanding of the problem and the users' needs. To achieve this, comprehensive research was conducted. That included literature research, interviews and data analysis, in order to gather insights and empathize with the experiences of international professionals and their onboarding challenges at SIMAC NL.

Literature Research

Photo by Kyle Glenn on Unsplash

During the empathize stage of the project, I undertook a comprehensive literature research, focusing on two key areas assigned to me based on the group's agreed task division. Firstly, I delved into the impact of the onboarding process on employee retention rates, seeking to understand the underlying factors that contribute to long-term commitment within a company. Surprisingly, the results revealed a strong correlation between effective onboarding and employees' decision to stay or leave within the first six months of employment. This finding highlighted the critical importance of a well-executed onboarding process.

Additionally, I conducted an analysis of SIMAC's platforms, particularly their LinkedIn profile and website. The aim was to uncover common words and phrases utilized by SIMAC in their social media and online presence. This analysis not only provided valuable insights into their content strategy but also served as a lens to understand how they engage with individuals who may be unfamiliar with the company.

Interviews

During a recent series of interviews conducted, we sought to gain insight into how employees perceive their onboarding experiences and the subsequent impact on their work. The interviews were organized in pairs, resulting in a total of six engaging discussions. Three interviewees were from various Dutch companies, while the remaining three were from Simac. Among the interviews I conducted, one particularly stood out. At Simac, my goal was to understand the employee's perspective on the onboarding process within the company. Surprisingly, the language barrier, which I initially presumed to be a significant challenge, was not an issue. The interviewee revealed that the company effortlessly switches to English when needed. In contrast, another interview left a lasting impression, even though I didn't personally conduct or analyze it. Denisy Cuna, the interviewee, emphasized the importance of socializing with colleagues outside of the workplace. According to her, this practice benefits both the company and employees, as it facilitates discussions about work in informal settings, enhances communication skills, and fosters meaningful connections among co-workers.

Define stage

In a dynamic group brainstorming session, our team embarked on a mission to generate a plethora of ideas by analyzing the insightful interviews we conducted. Employing the technique of affinity mapping, we collaboratively categorized our thoughts and observations, grouping them based on their relevance to one another. Sticky notes filled the walls as we uncovered patterns and connections among the various ideas. Ultimately, we employed a voting system to determine the most prominent categories. As a result, we decided to follow the captivating hook of socialization during the onboarding process, considering the consistent relevance and emphasis the interviewees expressed in our research. This choice promises to provide a fruitful direction for our future endeavors, as we recognize the transformative power of fostering connections among employees.

Ideate stage

Initial concept

Our first concept aimed to foster a deeper understanding of cultural differences within the company, while simultaneously encouraging employees to develop lasting relationships with the organization. The idea was to create an environment that promotes awareness and appreciation of diverse cultures, enabling employees to gain valuable insights into different perspectives. Additionally, we envisioned providing guidance and support to employees in achieving their personal and professional goals, emphasizing the company's commitment to their growth and development. By combining cultural awareness and individual support, our concept aimed to enhance employee engagement and create a strong sense of belonging within the organization.

A platform where new hires enhance their cultural differences alongside visualizing their backgrounds as well as forecasting and planning their career goals.

Sketching

In my sketches, I focused on designing a personal development plan as part of the concept mentioned earlier. The idea was to provide employees with a platform where they could outline their goals and aspirations, and receive feedback from their managers on their progress and areas for improvement.
However, after gathering feedback from stakeholders, this particular aspect of the concept was discarded. The stakeholders expressed some concerns about how the personal development plan seemed to veer more towards HR matters rather than being directly related to onboarding. Although initially disappointing, I saw this feedback as an opportunity for growth. It allowed me to build upon the criticism and develop a more creative and straightforward approach, which will be explored in the upcoming sections.

Concept iteration

Following the feedback from stakeholders, our project underwent a series of rapid iterations. Adopting a "pressure-cooker" approach, our group came together and, amidst the time constraints, critically assessed our previous research and brainstorming findings. It became evident that in order to truly emphasize socialization, which we recognized as the primary hook, we needed to place it at the heart of the concept. This led us to shift our focus from the initial individual-centric app to a more socially interactive platform. Through this process, we discovered that the concept that defines the entire project is fostering meaningful connections and fostering a sense of community within the organization.

Main concept:

A challenge-based app for new employees, fostering interaction and collaboration. It includes a world map highlighting cultural diversity within the company. The aim is to create an engaging and inclusive work environment.

Prototype stage

I was assigned to design the world map view as the homepage of the app. Opting for a minimalistic style, I incorporated smooth animations on the screen. The pin points on the map dynamically change as users scroll through the bottom cards. When reaching a specific card, a pop-up notification appears, presenting users with an intriguing daily challenge they are encouraged to follow.

Materialize

I conducted a concept validation testing session with Jenny Doan to assess the user understanding of the prototype design. The testing was divided into two sections: task-based assessment and usability evaluation. The former aimed to guide the tester in completing specific prompts, while the latter focused on the clarity of the app and the tester's experience while using it.
Key takeaways from the testing included Jenny rating the ease of navigating the product as 9 out of 10, a suggestion to incorporate click functionality in addition to scrolling on the map view, a desire for more engaging animations such as winning a trophy and spinning the wheel, and a strong endorsement from Jenny, stating she would definitely recommend the app to others.
